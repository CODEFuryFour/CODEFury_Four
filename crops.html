What is Crop Suitability?

Crop suitability refers to the assessment of specific crops' ability to grow optimally in a particular environment. Factors such as soil type, temperature, rainfall, and sunlight play a key role in determining crop compatibility.

Key Factors for Suitability:

  • Soil Type: Determines nutrient availability and drainage.
  • Climate: Includes temperature, rainfall, and humidity.
  • Sunlight: Impacts photosynthesis and growth.
